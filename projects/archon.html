Project

ARCHON

High Performance Infrastructure Platform

Operational / Active Development

Short Description

ARCHON is a purpose-built infrastructure platform designed from nearly a decade of lessons learned through server administration, enterprise networking, storage systems, and self-hosted services. It serves as the primary hosting environment for servers, websites, game services, automation systems, and future infrastructure experimentation.

Project Overview

ARCHON represents the second generation of my infrastructure philosophy. Unlike VEDA, which evolved gradually through years of upgrades and experimentation, ARCHON was designed intentionally from the ground up.

After purchasing my first home, I had the opportunity to build infrastructure before the walls were closed and before services were deployed. This allowed me to design networking, power, cabling, and rack systems around future requirements rather than adapting to existing limitations.

The platform is divided into two major subsystems: a dedicated network rack and a dedicated server rack. These systems are connected through a fiber backbone and designed around high-speed networking, UPS-backed operation, service isolation, and future expansion.

Today ARCHON hosts game servers, websites, smart-home infrastructure, community services, automation systems, and experimental platforms while serving as my primary environment for infrastructure development.

The Story of ARCHON

Starting Over

By the time I purchased my own house, I had already spent years maintaining VEDA and deploying enterprise networking systems. ARCHON was an opportunity to start fresh and apply everything I had learned from the beginning rather than evolving another system around old limitations.

Building the Backbone

Because the walls were still open during renovations, I installed shielded Cat6A cabling throughout the house and entertainment areas. Important systems such as computers and entertainment centers were placed on faster internal networking, while less demanding appliances and devices could remain on standard Gigabit infrastructure.

I also planned grounding and physical routing with future expansion in mind, giving the site a stronger foundation for future 10 Gigabit upgrades.

The Network Rack

The first subsystem was the network rack. This rack houses routing, switching, UPS systems, structured cabling, patching, web services, smart-home infrastructure, and network management hardware.

Fiber and 10 Gigabit Networking

A dedicated server rack was installed nearby and connected to the network rack through a 10 Gigabit fiber link. This separates networking responsibilities from compute and storage while maintaining high-speed internal connectivity.

Building the Server Platform

ARCHON became the new home for several services that originated in VEDA. Flauros continued as a dedicated Minecraft server, while additional systems such as Fenrik-2 and Marco-1 were added for Valheim and Terraria hosting.

The server naming convention was standardized into a name-and-revision format to make lifecycle tracking and future rebuilds easier to manage.

Current Role

ARCHON currently serves as my primary infrastructure platform. It hosts websites, game servers, automation systems, development environments, and community services while continuing to evolve as new requirements emerge.

Technical Challenges

  • Ground-up planning: designing infrastructure before construction was complete instead of adapting to an already finished space.
  • Structured cabling: installing shielded Cat6A while walls were open and planning cable paths around future service needs.
  • Fiber interconnect: linking the network rack and server rack through a high-speed fiber connection.
  • Power reliability: installing a dedicated circuit and UPS-backed operation from the beginning.
  • Service separation: dividing networking, compute, storage, and hosting responsibilities into more maintainable subsystems.
  • Multi-server hosting: supporting multiple game servers, websites, and internal services simultaneously.
  • Scalability: designing the platform around long-term expansion rather than immediate minimum requirements.
  • Security and maintainability: balancing remote access, self-hosting, convenience, privacy, and long-term reliability.

Miscellaneous Notes

ARCHON was intentionally designed to avoid many of the mistakes discovered throughout years of maintaining earlier infrastructure. Dedicated power, structured cabling, UPS protection, fiber connectivity, and service separation were all incorporated from the beginning rather than added later.

Unlike VEDA, which primarily represents the journey of learning infrastructure, ARCHON represents the application of those lessons into a purpose-built production environment.

The platform continues to evolve, but its core architecture reflects a more mature infrastructure philosophy focused on reliability, maintainability, and long-term scalability.
